## Content Opportunities for the Week Ahead

### Monday FinOps Insight

**"FinOps is no longer about cloud."** The State of FinOps 2026 mission update is the lead — the Foundation literally changed its name from "cloud" to "technology." The stat that carries it: two years ago, 31% of FinOps teams managed AI spend. Today: 98%. That's the fastest discipline expansion in the Foundation's history, and the implications (new skills needed, new tooling gaps, new stakeholder map) are squarely in your lane.

Secondary angle: the 90% scope expansion to SaaS, licensing, private cloud is the quiet story. FinOps practitioners are becoming technology controllers, not just cloud optimizers.

### Wednesday What I'm Watching

Two candidates — pick one:

**Option A: The inference cost collapse.** GPT-4 quality went from $30/million tokens to under $1 in roughly 30 months. Gartner says 90%+ more coming by 2030. The FinOps implication: your AI budget assumptions from 12 months ago are probably already wrong, and enterprises that locked in long-term AI contracts at 2024 pricing are going to feel it. What does re-procurement look like when costs are moving this fast?

**Option B: The OpenAI/NVIDIA 10 GW deal.** NVIDIA is now *investing* in OpenAI while OpenAI commits to buy 10 GW of NVIDIA hardware. That's a customer/investor relationship that doesn't have a clean parallel in tech history. What happens to OpenAI's pricing leverage if its silicon supplier owns equity in the company?
